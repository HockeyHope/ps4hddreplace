Step 1: Preperation

The first step is making sure you have everything you need

    Start State: You should already have a PS4, a controller and its charging cable. You will also need:

    A small Philips screwdriver

    The new hard drive that fits in PS4 (2.5 inch, 9.5 mm SATA II hard drive)

    Have a USB drive with enough space to back up your PS4 (the Systems Software is about 1 GB)

    End State: You have everything you need to replace the hard drive

Step 2: Backing It All Up

The second step is to get everything backed up to a safe spot.

    Start State: All the pieces are laid out in front of you

    Stick the USB drive into one of the PS4’s USB slots to copy the save data

    Go to Settings >> Application Saved Data Management >> Saved Data in System Storage >> Copy to USB Storage Device >> Select your game saves you want to transfer >> Copy

    Next you’ll need to go to Sony’s website here and download the System Software onto the USB drive (about 900 MB needed)

    End State: Everything is backed up

Step 3: Taking It Apart

The third step is to get the old hard drive pulled out of the PS4

    Start State: All supplies ready with the backed up USB drive

    First you will need to completely power off the PS4 and unplug it

    Gently slide off the top cover of the PS4

    Unscrew the single screw holding the hard drive case

    Unscrew the 4 screws of the hard drive cover to pull the hard drive out

    End State: Have all the pieces out and ready to put the new hard drive in

Step 4: Putting It Back Together

The fourth step is to put the new hard drive into the PS4

    Start State: You will have all the pieces you just unscrewed in front of you

    Replace casing with the new hard drive

    Screw the 4 screws back in

    Slide the cover back in and screw it back on securely

    Slide the top cover of the PS4 back on

    End State: PS4 ready to plug back in and turn on

Step 5: Getting it set back up

The final step is to have the PS4 ready to play with new hard drive

    Start State: PS4 with new hard drive in it and USB drive ready

    Plug PS4 back in and plug USB drive in it (plug controller directly into it as well)

    Turn it on by holding power button for 7 seconds (until it beeps 2 times)

    It will be in safe mode and select Initialize PS4 (Reinstall System Software)

    After it’s all rebooted. Sign back in. Go to Library and download your games back

    To recover saves from USB backup go to Application Saved Data Management >> Saved Data on USB Storage Device >> Copy to System Storage >> Select game >> Copy

CONGRADULATIONS! ENJOY YOUR NEW HARD DRIVE!
